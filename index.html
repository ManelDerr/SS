Who I am

Surgeon. Coach.
Mother. Seeker.

I am a holistic health coach, a maxillofacial surgeon, and a mother to two growing kids. I stepped into integrative wellness because I believe in the difference that truly holistic care can make — healing not just the body, but the mind, the nervous system, and the emotional self.

Why I became a surgeon

Health became important to me as a child when I witnessed how lives could be lost — and miraculously saved. That awe became a calling. I trained in maxillofacial surgery, and after nearly two decades in clinical practice, I can say there is nothing more fulfilling than the gratitude on a person's face when you've helped them heal — mentally, physically, or emotionally.

What made me make the switch

Having my first child changed my understanding of health completely. I was a doctor — and yet I had no idea how profoundly a dysregulated body could affect every area of life: energy, confidence, relationships, happiness. I trained in holistic nutrition, women's coaching, and fitness. And then life happened.

I had seen enough cancers, autoimmune diseases, and hormonal imbalances to know that 60–70% had an emotional or mental component being completely overlooked. I spiralled into anxiety, brain fog, and burnout. On the outside, I was doing everything right. But I had an emotional regulation gap — and no one had ever taught me to close it.

Learning to regulate my nervous system changed everything. Now I help women do the same.
